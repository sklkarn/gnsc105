    Example 6.3.15.

    What is the speed of wave which completes one full cycle in \(10 s\) and has a wavelength of \(120 m\text{?}\)
    Answer.
    \(v=12 m/s.\)
    Solution.
    Given: \(T=10 s\text{,}\) \(\lambda = 120 m\text{,}\) \(v=?\)
    From equation (6.1.2), we have
    \begin{align*} v \amp =f\lambda \end{align*}
    and from equation (6.1.1), we have
    \begin{align*} f \amp =\frac{1}{T} \\ \therefore v \amp =\frac{\lambda}{T} = \frac{120 m}{10 s} \end{align*}
