Earth Figure 11.0.1.(a)is the third planet from the sun and is the only known planet in the universe that supports life. It has a diameter of approximately 12,742 kilometers (7,918 miles) and a mass of approximately \(5.97 \times 10^24 \)kilograms. It has a unique atmosphere that is composed of 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and trace amounts of other gases. The planet's surface is covered by approximately 71% water, which is contained in oceans, lakes, and rivers, and the remaining 29% is composed of landmasses, such as continents and islands. The Earth is surrounded by a magnetic field, which protects it from harmful solar winds and radiation. The planet also has a natural satellite, the moon, which orbits around it. The Earth is a dynamic and constantly changing planet, with a complex system of tectonic plates that cause earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, and mountain building.
