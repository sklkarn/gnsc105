A fluid exerts more pressure at greater depths. Consider a cylindrical water bottle of area of cross-section \(A\text{.}\) If \(m\) is mass of water filled in the bottle to the height \(h\text{,}\) then from (4.2.2), we have -
\begin{equation*} p = \frac{F}{A} = \frac{mg}{A} =\frac{Vdg}{A} =\frac{Ahdg}{A} =hdg \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} p= hdg\tag{4.2.4} \end{equation}
Here, \(V\) is the volume of fluid in a container, \(d\) is the density of the fluid, and \(g\) is acceleration due to gravity.
