Let us apply Newton's II law into II equation of motion and rearange them.
\begin{align*} v^2_f \amp = v^2_i + 2 a d\\ or, v^2_f \amp = v^2_i + 2 \frac{F}{m} d\\ or, v^2_f - v^2_i \amp = 2 \frac{Fd}{m} \\ or, m\left(v^2_f - v^2_i\right) \amp = 2 F d\\ or, \frac{1}{2} \left(mv^2_f - mv^2_i\right)\amp = Fd\\ or, \frac{1}{2}mv^2_f - \frac{1}{2}mv^2_i\amp = W\\ \therefore W \amp = \frac{1}{2}mv^2_f - \frac{1}{2}mv^2_i \end{align*}
Here, \(\frac{1}{2}mv^2_f \) is final kinetic energy and \(\frac{1}{2}mv^2_i \) is initial kinetic energy. Hence, \(\frac{1}{2}mv^2_f - \frac{1}{2}mv^2_i\) is change in kinetic energy. Therefore, work done = change in kinetic energy. This is known as work-energy theorem. Meaning the amount of work is done on a body is equal to its change in kinetic energy or vice a versa along the straight line.
