Gamma (\(\gamma\)) rays emission: \(\gamma\) ray is an electromagnetic radiation of very high energy and is neutral to the charge. If the nucleus is unstable due to access amount of energy then it releases \(\gamma\) rays. The composition of nucleus does not change due to gamma radiation emissioin. For example: high energetic Pb turns into low energetic Pb in gamma radiation:
\begin{equation*} {}_{82}^{204}Pb^* \longrightarrow {}_{82}^{204}Pb + \gamma \end{equation*}
 1  Gamma rays have no rest mass and travel with the velocity of light and hence penetrate even thick plate of lead Figure 7.2.2.
