Crystalline Solids: These are solids with a regular, repeating pattern of atoms, molecules or ions arranged in a three-dimensional lattice structure. The lattice structure gives the solid its characteristic properties, such as melting point, hardness, and conductivity. Crystalline solids can be further classified into two subtypes: molecular crystals, where the repeating units are molecules, and atomic crystals, where the repeating units are atoms.
