Acids and bases are two types of chemical compounds that have unique properties and behavior. Acids are compounds that have a high concentration of hydrogen ions \((H^+)\text{,}\) which they release when they are dissolved in water. Acid tests sour.  1  Orange, lemon, tomato, tamarind, and grapefruit are sour and contain acid in their juice. Bases, on the other hand, have a high concentration of hydroxide ions \((OH^-)\text{,}\) which they release when they are dissolved in water. Base also known as Alkali tests bitter. Cucumber, asparagus, garlic, yam, broccoli, and fish slime are alkaline.
\begin{equation*} HCl \longrightarrow H^+ +Cl^- \text{dissociation of hydrochloric acid} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} H_2SO_4 \longrightarrow H^+ +SO_4^- \text{dissociation of sulfuric acid} \end{equation*}
Acid dissociates into hydrogen ion when dissolve in water.
