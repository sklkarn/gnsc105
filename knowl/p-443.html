Dalton's atomic model was proposed in 1803. It tells that
  1. All matter is composed of atoms, which are indivisible and indestructible.
  2. Atoms of a given element are identical in size, mass, and other properties.
  3. Atoms of different elements have different properties and masses.
  4. Atoms combine in simple whole-number ratios to form compounds.
In chemical reactions, atoms are rearranged to form new compounds, but no atoms are created or destroyed. Dalton's atomic theory laid the foundation for modern atomic theory.
