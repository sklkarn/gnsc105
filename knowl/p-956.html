A galaxy is a massive system of stars, gas, dust, and other celestial objects that are held together by gravity. Galaxies come in a variety of shapes and sizes [FigureÂ 14.1.1.(a)], ranging from small dwarf galaxies to giant elliptical galaxies. The Milky Way, which is the galaxy that contains our solar system, is a barred spiral galaxy with a diameter of approximately 100,000 light-years and contains hundreds of billions of stars. Galaxies are the building blocks of the universe, and studying them can provide insights into the evolution and structure of the cosmos.
