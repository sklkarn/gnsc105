Alpha (\(\alpha\)) particle emission: \(\alpha\) particle is a helium nucleus and is positively charged particle. In \(\alpha\) decay a nucleus emits an alpha particle, which consists of two protons and two neutrons. For example: thorium (Th) decayes into radium (Ra) by emitting alpha particle:
\begin{equation*} {}_{90}^{232}Th \longrightarrow {}_{88}^{228}Ra + {}_2^{4}He \end{equation*}
This results in a decrease in the atomic number of the nucleus by two and transforms the nucleus into a different element. Alpha decay occurs when the nucleus has too many protons and is not stable, and it releases energy in the form of the alpha particle to reach a more stable configuration.
