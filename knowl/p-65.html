Objects moving under the influence of gravity only is called a free fall. This means gravity causes a change in the velocity of any object by 9.80 \(m/s\) every second. So, drop any object and let it fall toward the ground: After 1s, the speed of the object is 9.80 \(m/s\text{.}\) After 2s, the speed of the object is 19.6 \(m/s\text{.}\) Etc. According to Galileo the velocity of a falling object is proportional to the length of time that the object has been falling. From the first equation of motion \(v_f-v_i=gt\) or, \(v=gt, \)
\begin{equation*} \quad \therefore v\propto t, \end{equation*}
as \(g\) is a constant. Hence, the object is falling with higher velocity if it is falling for a long time or from a higher position. Since \(g\) is constant near the planet all objects fall at the same rate. (Assuming there is no air resistance).
