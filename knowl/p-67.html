Objects moving under the influence of only gravity is called a free fall. According to Galileo the velocity of a falling object is proportional to the length of time that the object has been falling. From the first equation of motion \(v_f-v_i=gt\text{.}\) \(v=gt, \quad \therefore v\propto t, \quad \) as \(g\) is constant. Hence, the object is falling with higher velocity if it is falling from longer time or higher position. Since \(g\) is constant all objects fall at the same rate. (Assuming there is no air drag).
