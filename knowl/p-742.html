A nonpolar molecule has no separation of charge, meaning that the electrons are shared equally between the atoms in the molecule. This occurs when the electronegativity difference between the atoms in the molecule is zero or very small. Examples of nonpolar molecules include: carbon dioxide \((CO_2)\text{,}\) methane \((CH_4)\text{,}\) ethane \((C_2H_6)\text{,}\) nitrogen gas \((N_2)\text{,}\) oxygen gas \((O_2)\text{,}\) benzene \((C_6H_6)\text{.}\) These molecules do not have a positive or negative pole, which means that they do not have a permanent dipole moment.
