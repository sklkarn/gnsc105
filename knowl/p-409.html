Electrical Shock: According to Ohm's law if resistance of the conductor is low then the higher current can flow through it. High current can be deadly. Actually, a “voltage” does not go “into” your body rather current can pass through it when charges flow toward a lower potential. In order to get electric shock atleast \(1mA\) of current must pass through our body. In dry condition, human body has a resistance of about \(50000\Omega\text{,}\) so to have \(1mA\) current flowing through our body we need to touch the voltage source of about \(50 V\text{,}\) as shown in the calculation below.
\begin{align*} V\amp =IR \\ or, \quad V \amp = 1\times 10^{-3}\times 50000\\ \therefore \quad V \amp =50 \end{align*}
In wet condition, human body resistance becomes only \(10000\Omega\text{,}\) so to get \(1mA\) current through our body we need to touch the voltage source of about \(10 V\text{.}\)
