Electron (\(e^{-1}\)) Capture: it is another type of radioactive decay in which an inner orbital electron is captured by the nucleus and transforms into a neutron, resulting in a decrease in the atomic number of the nucleus by one and the transformation of the nucleus into a different element. The captured electron combines with a proton to form a neutron, which remains inside the nucleus. This process releases energy in the form of gamma rays or X-rays. Electron capture is different from beta decay, which involves the emission of a beta particle (an electron or a positron).
