The Andromeda Galaxy [FigureÂ 14.1.1.(c)], also known as Messier 31 or M31, is a spiral galaxy located about 2.5 million light-years away from Earth in the constellation Andromeda. It is the closest spiral galaxy to our own Milky Way. The Andromeda Galaxy is about 220,000 light-years in diameter, making it almost twice as large as the Milky Way. It contains about a trillion stars, which is more than three times the number of stars in the Milky Way. Like the Milky Way, it has a central bulge and spiral arms that wrap around it.
