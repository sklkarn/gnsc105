Angular momentum is the momentum associated with a body in rotation about some axis. It is also a vector quantity. The direction of angular momentum is parallel to the axis of rotation. Spinning ball or rotating wheel has an angular momentum. Angular momentum is represented by, \(L\text{.}\) Angular momentum is defined by \(L=I\omega\text{.}\) Where \(I\) is called moment of inertia and \(\omega\) is called the rotational velocity of the object. Moment of inertia, \(I\) tells us that how the mass of a body is distributed relative to the axis of rotation. It is the property of a material that restrict it to have any change in its rotation state of motion.
