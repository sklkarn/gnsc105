Electron (\(e^{-}\)) Capture: it is another type of radioactive decay in which an inner orbital electron is captured by the nucleus and transforms into a neutron, resulting in a decrease in the atomic number of the nucleus by one and the transformation of the nucleus into a different element. For example: flourine (F) turns into oxygen (O) due to positron emission:
\begin{equation*} {}_{1}^{1}p + {}_{-1}^{0}e \longrightarrow {}_{0}^{1}n + \nu \end{equation*}
Another example is
\begin{equation*} {}_{92}^{231}U + {}^0_{-1}e \longrightarrow {}_{91}^{231}Pa + \nu \end{equation*}
The captured electron combines with a proton to form a neutron, which remains inside the nucleus. This process releases energy in the form of gamma rays or X-rays. Electron capture is different from beta decay, which involves the emission of a beta particle (an electron or a positron).
