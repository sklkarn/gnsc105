    Example 3.3.0.1.

    A moving object does not necessarily have
    1. momentum.
    2. velocity.
    3. kinetic energy.
    4. potential energy.
    Hint.
    Think of what if the object is moving on the ground.
    Solution.
    d.

    Example 3.3.0.2.

    What happens when two or more objects collide
    1. the total momentum remains the same of all the objects.
    2. the total kinetic energy remains the same of all the objects.
    3. the momentum of each object never changes.
    4. the kinetic energy of each object never changes.
    Hint.
    Read the conservation principles.
    Solution.
    a.

    Example 3.3.0.3.

    You are dropping a golf ball and a ping-pong ball from a high tower on the surface of a planet which has no air. Which of the following quantities remain the same when they have fallen halfway down?
    1. kinetic energy.
    2. potential energy.
    3. speed.
    4. acceleration due to gravity.
    Hint.
    Read section Subsection 2.1.2
    Solution.
    c. and d.

    Example 3.3.0.4.

    Find the work done by a force if you are holding a 5 kg object 2m above the ground for 40 second.
    Hint.
    Read section Section 3.1
    Answer.
    0.
    Solution.
    Force has not displaced the object, so distance =0. Hence \(W=fd=0.\)

    Example 3.3.0.5.

    If the kinetic energy of a bullet of mass 400 g is 50kJ. Find its velocity.
    Solution.
    Given: m=400g = 0.4 kg, KE=50 kJ = \(50\times 1000 J = 50000J, v=?\)
    \begin{align*} KE \amp =\frac{1}{2}mv^2\\ 50000 \amp =\frac{1}{2}\times 0.4 \times v^2\\ v^2 \amp =\frac{50000\times 2}{0.4}\\ \therefore v \amp =\sqrt{250000} = 500 \end{align*}
    Answer.
    500 m/s.

    Example 3.3.0.6.

    Find the linear momentum of a 50 kg dog running at 5 m/s.
    Solution.
    Given: m=50 kg, v=5 \(m/s,\) p =?
    \begin{align*} p \amp =mv\\ p \amp =50\times 5\\ \therefore p \amp =250 \end{align*}
    Answer.
    \(250 kgm/s.\)

    Example 3.3.0.7.

    Find the recoil velocity of a rocket of mass 100kg when it ejects 50 g of gas from its propulsion pistol at a speed of 50 m/s.
    Hint.
    Use conservation of momentum principle.
    Solution.
    Given:\(m_1=100 kg, m_2= 50 g=0.05kg, v_1= ?, v_2=50 m/s.\)
    \begin{align*} p_1\amp =p_2\\ or, m_1v_1\amp =m_2v_2\\ or, v_2\amp =\frac {m_2v_2}{m_1} \\ \therefore v_2\amp =\frac {0.05\times 50}{100} = 0.025 m/s \end{align*}
